# Story Spine — Quick Reference
*Terra Studio Week 4: Storytelling and AI — [terra.do](https://terra.do)*

## The Six Beats

```
Once upon a time...   → Who is the protagonist? What is the status quo?
Every day...          → What was normal? What systems or constraints defined the situation?
One day...            → What changed? The inciting incident or discovery that broke the status quo.
Because of that...    → First consequence. Must be causal, not just chronological.
Because of that...    → Second consequence. Escalate stakes or show transformation.
Until finally...      → The resolution. What must this specific audience feel, decide, or do?
```

**The test:** Between each beat, ask: "and then" or "because of that"?
If "and then" fits → no spine. Restructure.

---

## The "Because of That" Test

Read any communication — report, brief, pitch — point by point.

| Connector | What it means |
|-----------|---------------|
| "And then" fits | Points are parallel. It's a list. Any point could be cut. Audience learns. |
| "Because of that" fits | Points are causal. Structure creates momentum. Audience is transported. |

Anywhere "and then" appears is a weak link. That is where audiences check out.

---

## The Bullet Point Diagnostic (5 Questions)

- [ ] **Struggle** — Is there an obstacle? What happens if nothing changes?
- [ ] **Causal chain** — Can you replace "and then" with "because of that" throughout?
- [ ] **Defined ending** — Does the audience have a specific action or feeling to land on?
- [ ] **Ruthless focus** — Does every element serve the narrative, or do details dilute it?
- [ ] **Transportation** — Will they *feel* something, or just *learn* something?

If you check fewer than 3: restructure using the Story Spine before sending.

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Hero metric as headline | Move it inside the causal chain as a consequence |
| Generic "Until finally" | Ask: what does *this* audience need to feel or decide specifically? |
| "One day" as a transition | Make it a revelation — something the audience didn't expect to find |
| Two "Because of that" beats that don't escalate | Beat 5 should raise the stakes above beat 4, not restate it |
| Vague consequences | Name the number, the place, the person, the deadline |

## Audience-Specific Endings

Same facts. Different audiences. Different resolutions.

> **Minister of environment:** "The question is whether this ministry will fund expanding the system to the countries where forests are still disappearing without any real-time monitoring."

> **Foundation program officer:** "The question is whether this grant goes to the 12 countries that have the data, the enforcement agencies, and no early-warning system."

> **City council:** "The question before this council is whether we accept that where you live determines whether summer heat is an inconvenience or a death sentence."

The facts don't change. The "Until finally" does.

## Quick Prompts for Claude / ChatGPT

**To restructure a brief:**
> "Apply the Pixar Story Spine to this policy brief. Identify the 'One day' moment, place the hero metric as a consequence in the causal chain, and tailor the 'Until finally' beat to [your audience]. Then run the 'because of that' test and flag any weak links."

**To test a draft:**
> "Read this draft and apply the 'because of that' test between each major point. Identify where 'and then' could substitute, and suggest how to strengthen those weak links causally."

**To get feedback on a Story Spine:**
> "Evaluate this Story Spine on four criteria: (1) causal chain — do the beats connect causally or chronologically? (2) stakes — is the consequence of inaction clear? (3) specificity — are consequences named and concrete? (4) resolution — does 'Until finally' land on a specific audience action or feeling?"
