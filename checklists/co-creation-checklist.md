# AI Co-Creation Checklist
**Terra Studio — Week 4: Storytelling and AI**

---

## The Four-Step Workflow

| Step | Owner | What happens |
|------|-------|--------------|
| **Step 1** | You | Define the spec: audience, value frame, hero metric, distance strategy, Story Spine |
| **Step 2** | AI | Generate a raw draft from your complete spec |
| **Step 3** | You | Curate and fact-check — verify every specific claim against source material |
| **Step 4** | You + AI | Multiply: adapt the spec for additional audiences and formats |

**The rule:** AI accelerates execution. You define intent. Never delegate Step 1.

---

## The Seven Step 1 Questions

Before generating any draft, answer all seven. Gaps here become failures in the output.

1. **Who exactly is the audience?**
   Role, institution, what they already know, what decision they need to make.

2. **What do they already believe about this topic?**
   Assumptions, existing mental models, and barriers to reception.

3. **What single value frame will this audience respond to — and why?**
   Choose one: Economic / Public Health / Conservation & Stewardship / National Security / Jobs & Local Economy / Faith & Moral Duty

4. **What is the single most concrete, credible metric that will anchor the story?**
   Specific number, unit, source, and year. Not "significant increase" — a number.

5. **What is the primary psychological distance barrier?**
   Geographic (feels far away) / Temporal (feels like the future) / Social (someone else's problem) / Uncertainty (science isn't settled)

6. **How specifically will the draft collapse that distance?**
   Local data / Present tense / Personal story / Analogous risk / Institutional source

7. **What is the exact call to action?**
   The specific decision this audience can make. Not "take action" — a vote, a budget line, a meeting, a sign-up.

---

## The Five-Point Output Test

Apply to every AI-generated draft before editing or publishing.

| # | Criterion | Check |
|---|-----------|-------|
| 1 | **Audience specificity** | Is there a named, specific audience — or generic "the public"? |
| 2 | **Value frame** | Does the opening activate the audience's own values, or lead with "climate" in a way that triggers identity resistance? |
| 3 | **Hero metric** | Is there one concrete, sourced statistic anchoring the story? Or are all claims impressionistic? |
| 4 | **Distance collapsed** | Is the problem local, present, and personal — or distant in time, place, or social identity? |
| 5 | **Call to action** | Does the ending give THIS audience a concrete decision they control — or a generic "we must act"? |

**Scoring:** Pass all five = ready to edit. Fail two or more = revise the brief, not the draft.

## Value Frame Quick Reference

| Frame | Audience | Entry point | Vocabulary to use |
|-------|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Economic** | CFOs, investors, planners | Cost, liability, market opportunity | Liability, ROI, exposure, risk rating |
| **Public Health** | Health advocates, parents, providers | Clean air, hospital costs, children | Preventable, burden, mortality, ER visits |
| **Conservation & Stewardship** | Traditional, rural, faith communities | Responsible land management, not wasting | Stewardship, inheritance, responsible management |
| **National Security** | Military, policy planners | Energy independence, resilience | Vulnerability, operational, supply chain |
| **Jobs & Local Economy** | Rural, manufacturing, small business | Employment, community investment | Jobs, local, manufacturing, income |
| **Faith & Moral Duty** | Faith communities | Creation care, intergenerational responsibility | Stewardship, obligation, future generations |

**Rule:** Never use the frame the audience didn't choose. Don't lead with "environment" for an economic audience.

## Four Psychological Distances

| Distance | Description | Collapsing strategy |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Geographic** | "Happens somewhere else" | Local county/city data, name the street |
| **Temporal** | "Happens in the future" | "Already happening" + present-tense data |
| **Social** | "Happens to other people" | Personal story from this community |
| **Uncertainty** | "Science isn't clear enough to act" | Analogous risk they already manage |

## The Story Spine (Pixar structure)

```
Once upon a time, [audience's world and baseline assumption]...
Every day, [what they considered normal]...
Until one day, [the disruption or finding — introduce hero metric here]...
Because of that, [first consequence]...
Because of that, [second consequence — often the economic/health impact]...
Until finally, [the specific decision or action this audience controls]...
```

**Key rules:**
- Hero metric appears as a *consequence* in the causal chain — not as the headline
- "Until finally" gives this specific audience a concrete action — not a generic positive statement
- Do not flatten beats into a bullet list — the causal chain is the structure

---

## Common Failure Modes

| Failure | What it looks like | Fix |
|---------|--------------------|-----|
| No audience specified | "Communities everywhere..." | Name role, institution, decision context |
| Wrong value frame | "Climate emergency" to a CFO | Check audience values before choosing frame |
| No hero metric | "Significant increase..." | Find one specific number with source and year |
| Distance maximized | "Global crisis," "future generations" | Local data, present tense, named person |
| Fragmented call to action | "We need: bullets 1–6" | One specific decision this audience owns |
| Story spine missing | Parallel facts instead of causal chain | Map six beats before generating |
