# Audience Diagnostic Checklist
*Terra Studio Week 4: Storytelling and AI — [terra.do](https://terra.do)*

## The Seven Pre-Flight Questions

Before drafting any climate communication — pitch, brief, email, slide deck:

```
☐ 01 — Audience specificity   Who exactly is my audience? "The public" is never the answer.
☐ 02 — Value frame             What do they value? Economic? Health? Security? Stewardship? Justice? Local pride?
☐ 03 — Psychological distance  Which distance dimension is the primary barrier?
☐ 04 — Collapsing strategy     How will I make this feel local, present, personal, and concrete?
☐ 05 — Messenger fit           Am I the credible voice here, or do I need to equip someone who is?
☐ 06 — Fear + agency           Does my story include both the threat AND a concrete path to action?
☐ 07 — Role for the audience   Does my ending give them a specific thing to do, not understanding alone?
```

If the communication passes narrative structure (Module 1 Story Spine) AND all seven of these, it is ready for production.

## The Four Psychological Distances

Each dimension independently reduces urgency. Collapsing one does not collapse the others.

| Distance | What it sounds like | Collapsing strategy |
|----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Temporal** | "This is a future problem." | Present-tense evidence. What happened last summer, last month, last year. |
| **Spatial** | "It's happening somewhere else." | Local hooks. Name the neighborhood, the city, the watershed. |
| **Social** | "It's not affecting people like me." | Relatable characters. A farmer in your county. A school nurse tracking asthma. |
| **Hypothetical** | "It might not happen." | Concrete evidence. Insurance premiums rising. Infrastructure budgets already strained. |

---

## The Four Value Frames

Same fact. Four different doors. Each activates what the audience already values.

| Frame | Audience | Entry language |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| **Conservation / Stewardship** | Conservative / traditional values | Avoiding wastefulness, responsible land management, obligation to future generations |
| **Public Health** | Community / health advocates | Clean air, childhood asthma, hospital costs, preventable illness, heat-related mortality |
| **Economic** | Business, investors, city CFOs | Cost reduction, market opportunity, infrastructure liability, risk management, balance sheets |
| **National Security** | Military planners, policymakers | Energy independence, supply chain resilience, strategic vulnerability, resource competition |

**Rule:** Do not lead with "climate change" or "the environment" if the audience might filter those labels through political identity. Find the door they will open.

---

## Fear + Agency: The Calibration

Fear works when four components are present:

1. **Severity** — How bad is the threat?
2. **Susceptibility** — Could it happen to me?
3. **Response efficacy** — Would the recommended action actually help?
4. **Self-efficacy** — Can I actually do it?

When efficacy is missing, fear produces paralysis or denial — not action. This is the mechanism behind doomerism: it delivers severity and susceptibility without showing a path forward.

**The sequence matters:** Fear → Agency. A narrative that moves from struggle to resolution (the Story Spine) is psychologically calibrated. A narrative that delivers fear without agency damages it.

## Common Diagnostic Failures

| Failure | What it looks like | Fix |
|---------|--------------------|-----|
| Generic audience | "Policy makers," "the public," "decision makers" | Name a specific group with specific accountability and specific values |
| Wrong door | Leads with "climate change" for a conservative audience | Find the value frame they will open — stewardship, fiscal, health, security |
| Unaddressed distance | Global statistics, 2050 projections | Replace one global fact with one local, current, named data point |
| Fear without agency | Ends with "we're running out of time" | Add: what this specific audience can do, in this term, with tools they control |
| Generic ending | "We must act on climate change" | Name one decision, one vote, one allocation — what does THIS audience do? |

## Quick Prompts for Claude / ChatGPT

**To run a full diagnostic:**
> "Diagnose this climate communication using the 7-question Audience Analysis Framework. For each dimension — audience specificity, value frame, psychological distance, collapsing strategy, messenger fit, fear+agency balance, and role for the audience — give a one-sentence verdict and one specific improvement suggestion. My target audience is [describe audience]."

**To test a value frame:**
> "Does this communication use the audience's own value vocabulary — economic, health, security, stewardship — or does it lead with 'climate' or 'environment' in ways that might trigger identity resistance? Identify the dominant frame and suggest an alternative entry point for a [conservative / business / health / security] audience."

**To collapse a specific distance:**
> "The primary psychological distance barrier for my audience is [temporal / spatial / social / hypothetical]. Suggest three specific ways to collapse it using local, present, personal, or concrete evidence from [topic / dataset / region]."
