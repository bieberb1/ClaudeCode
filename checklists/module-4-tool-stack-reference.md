# Module 4: Tool Stack Reference
**Terra Studio Week 4 — Storytelling and AI**

---

## The organizing principle

The AI tool landscape changes monthly. Individual tools rise and fall. The categories of what they produce are stable. Organize by output type — when a tool gets replaced by something better, you know which category to shop in.

**Before choosing any tool, answer four questions:**
1. What format? (email, brief, deck, card, video, interactive)
2. Who's the audience?
3. What are the stakes?
4. Are you exploring or executing?

---

## Seven output categories

### 1. Text & Reasoning
*Drafts, pitches, emails, policy briefs*

| Tool | Tier | Use for |
|------|------|---------|
| **Claude** (Projects) | Recommended | Persistent strategic context — load your Story Spine, audience checklist, value frame guide once and they stay loaded. All four workflow steps. |
| ChatGPT (Custom GPTs) | Alternative | Equivalent persistent context via Custom GPTs. Stay here if already established in the OpenAI ecosystem. |
| Gemini (Gems) | Alternative | Tight Google Drive / Workspace integration. Use when source material lives in Google. |

**Principle:** Build a dedicated storytelling workspace with your frameworks loaded as permanent context. The brand matters less than the habit.

---

### 2. Research
*Evidence gathering, hero metric sourcing, document synthesis*

| Tool | Tier | Use for |
|------|------|---------|
| **Perplexity** | Recommended | Outward search — decompose questions into sub-searches, get structured briefings with traceable citations. Step 1 evidence base. |
| **NotebookLM** | Recommended | Inward search — synthesize reports and PDFs you already have. Directed Audio Overview, Study Guide, and Briefing Doc for narrative hooks. |

**When to use which:**
- Perplexity answers "What does the world know about this topic?" (searches outward)
- NotebookLM answers "What's hiding in the documents I already have?" (searches inward)

Common workflow: Perplexity first → export briefing as PDF → upload into NotebookLM alongside other sources.

---

### 3. Visual & Design
*Social cards, report graphics, concept imagery*

| Tool | Tier | Use for |
|------|------|---------|
| **Google Flow** | Recommended | Early-stage exploration. Generate images (Imagen), animate to video (Veo), explore visual directions before committing. Free to start. |
| **Canva AI** | Recommended | Polished production. Finished assets: social cards, report covers, presentation graphics. Bulk Create for localized scale. |

**Workflow:** Flow first to explore → Canva to execute. Skip Flow for routine work where the direction is already clear.

**Honest assessment:** AI image generation excels at visualizing futures and scenarios. For factual documentation of real places, use real photography.

---

### 4. Video & Audio
*Scenario clips, B-roll, narration*

| Tool | Tier | Use for |
|------|------|---------|
| **Google Veo** (via Flow / Gemini) | Recommended | Atmospheric scenario visualization. Generate concept image → animate in same workspace. Temporal distance collapsing. |
| **ElevenLabs** | Alternative | Voice generation and narration. Claude (script) → ElevenLabs (narrate) → Veo (video) is a complete pipeline. |
| Kling | Alternative | Higher-volume video generation when Veo limits feel restrictive. |

**Audio as output:** NotebookLM Audio Overviews and ElevenLabs narration are underestimated as final outputs. A 5-minute narrated data story can reach audiences that would never open a PDF.

---

### 5. Presentations
*Slide decks, structured pitch materials*

| Tool | Tier | Use for |
|------|------|---------|
| **Claude to Slides** | Recommended | Generate a structured deck from your Story Spine. More control — frameworks baked into the generation step. Output as Google Slides markdown or PowerPoint outline. |
| Gamma | Alternative | Fastest path to a designed first draft. Paste your Story Spine and Gamma builds a full deck in minutes. Treat output as a starting point. |

**Tradeoff:** Gamma is faster for a rough first pass. Claude-to-slides produces output that needs less redesign because you've specified your constraints upfront.

---

### 6. Interactive Tools
*Calculators, explorers, scenario tools*

| Tool | Tier | Use for |
|------|------|---------|
| **Claude Artifacts** | Recommended | Generate interactive React components inside your Claude conversation. Prototyping and internal use. Zero additional setup. |
| **Google Opal** | Recommended | Describe what you want → Opal builds a shareable public link. Simple public-facing tools. |
| Lovable / Replit | Advanced | Complex applications you intend to maintain and update. Multi-page calculators, tools that pull from live APIs. |

**Decision framework:**
- Artifacts: prototyping, internal use (fastest)
- Opal: simple shareable public tools
- Lovable/Replit: complex applications with ongoing maintenance

**Reserve interactive tools for high-stakes, high-reach communications.** A city council presentation may warrant a risk explorer. An internal team update probably does not.

---

### 7. Content Multiplication
*Clip extraction, transcript editing, localized asset generation*

| Tool | Tier | Use for |
|------|------|---------|
| **OpusClip** | Recommended | Upload long-form video → AI identifies engaging moments → reframes for vertical → adds captions. One 60-minute webinar = a month of social content. Free tier available. |
| **Descript** | Recommended | Edit video by editing text. Transcript-based editorial control. Free tier sufficient to test. |
| BannerBear / Creatomate | Alternative | Template automation at scale. Design once → auto-generate hundreds of localized versions (different cities, stats, dates). |

---

## The multiplication chain

**One source → many outputs. Evidence base stays constant. Format and frame adapt per audience.**

Example — Danny Kennedy keynote on solar energy transition:
1. **Descript:** Edit full recording into 10-minute highlight reel (economic argument)
2. **OpusClip:** Extract 6-8 vertical clips for social
3. **Claude:** Blog post + LinkedIn thread + newsletter excerpt + policy brief (each for a different audience)
4. **NotebookLM:** Directed Audio Overview to surface narrative hooks

One keynote → 30+ pieces of content. Each reaching a different audience through a different channel.

## Quick decision matrix

| I need to... | Tool |
|---|---|
| Draft a pitch with my frameworks already loaded | Claude Project / Custom GPT / Gemini Gem |
| Find the hero metric for my topic | Perplexity |
| Synthesize PDFs I already have | NotebookLM |
| Explore what a campaign could look like visually | Google Flow |
| Produce a final social card | Canva AI |
| Make a climate scenario visible | Google Veo (via Flow) |
| Generate a slide deck from my Story Spine | Claude to Slides |
| Prototype an interactive calculator | Claude Artifacts |
| Build a shareable public tool | Google Opal |
| Extract clips from a keynote | OpusClip or Descript |
